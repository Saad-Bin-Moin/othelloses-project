Cybersecurity Engineer

A Cybersecurity Engineer is responsible for protecting an organization's network, systems, and data from cyber threats. Their role includes designing and implementing security measures, monitoring systems for suspicious activity, and responding to security incidents. They also perform risk assessments and ensure compliance with security standards to prevent data breaches and cyberattacks.

Abilities of a Cybersecurity Engineer

Full-Time Cybersecurity Engineer Capabilities

Analyzing and identifying potential security threats to the network and systems, and implementing preventive measures to reduce risks.
Configuring and maintaining firewalls and intrusion detection systems to monitor and protect the network from external threats.
Responding to security breaches, identifying the root cause, and implementing remediation strategies to restore systems to a secure state.

Dispatch Cybersecurity Engineer Capabilities

Responding promptly to security incidents, mitigating immediate threats, and ensuring continuity of critical systems during a breach.
Conducting on-site security audits, risk assessments, and penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities and enhance the security posture.
Installing and configuring advanced cybersecurity tools such as SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) and endpoint protection systems.
